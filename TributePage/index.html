Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man Of India

About The Legend

    Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, commonly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an eminent Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he emerged as a prominent figure known for his contributions to India's space and missile development programs. Kalam began his career as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO), where he played a pivotal role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle and various missiles. He was particularly instrumental in the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and the Agni and Prithvi missiles. Apart from his scientific pursuits, Kalam was a visionary leader who emphasized the importance of education and youth empowerment. He believed in harnessing the potential of India's youth for national development and prosperity. He often interacted with students and encouraged them to pursue careers in science and technology. Kalam's presidency was marked by his dedication to promoting education, scientific research, and societal development. He was widely respected for his humility, integrity, and unwavering commitment to the welfare of the nation. After his presidency, Kalam continued to be actively involved in various educational and societal initiatives. He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which inspired millions of people, especially the youth, to strive for excellence and innovation. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He left behind a legacy of scientific achievement, patriotism, and a vision for a developed and empowered India. Kalam remains a revered figure, admired for his remarkable contributions to India's progress and his profound impact on the lives of countless individuals.

    Here's a Time-line of his Life..

    • 1931 Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu.

    • 1960 Graduated in Aerospace Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.

    • 1969 Joined Space Research at ISRO.

    • 1980 Became the project Director for India's First Indegenous Satellite Program.

    • 1980 Was involved in the development of several Indegenous Missiles for India like Agni, Prithvi.

    • 1981 Awarded Padma Bhushan.

    • 1990 Awarded Padma Vibhushan.

    • 1992 Chief Advisor for the India's Nuclear Program.

    • 1997 Awarded Bharat Ratna.

    • 2002 Became 11th President of India.

    • 2015 The People's President passes away while doing what he loved the most, addressing students.
